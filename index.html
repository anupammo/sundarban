The monsoon season (June to September) brings heavy rainfall, making the area less accessible, but it also transforms the landscape into a lush, green paradise.

Why Choose Sundarban for Your Next Adventure?

The Sundarban is the largest mangrove forest in the world, stretching across India and Bangladesh. This vast wilderness is home to a rich variety of flora and fauna, including over 400 species of wildlife. The most famous resident, of course, is the Royal Bengal Tiger, but the Sundarban also boasts an array of other wildlife, such as saltwater crocodiles, spotted deer, wild boars, and numerous bird species.

Explore Sundarban's Unique Attractions

Sundarban National Park

The heart of the Sundarban, this national park is home to a vast array of wildlife, including the Royal Bengal Tiger. Explore the park's waterways on a boat safari and immerse yourself in the natural beauty of the mangroves.

Sajnekhali Watchtower

One of the best spots for wildlife viewing, the Sajnekhali Watchtower offers a chance to see tigers, deer, and a variety of birds. The tower is also home to a small museum and a crocodile park.

Dobanki Watchtower

This watchtower features a canopy walk that takes you above the forest, offering a bird's-eye view of the surroundings. It's a great spot for photography and bird watching.
