Introduction to Dobanki Watchtower

The Sundarban National Park is famous for its dense mangrove forests, rich biodiversity, and the majestic Royal Bengal Tiger. Among the numerous watchtowers that provide a glimpse into this natural treasure trove, Dobanki Watchtower stands out as one of the most thrilling experiences in Sundarban. Known for its unique canopy walk, Dobanki offers visitors a rare opportunity to explore the wildlife and vegetation from an elevated perspective, all while walking through the treetops.

Birdwatchers will also find Dobanki to be a paradise, with species like storks, egrets, and kingfishers frequently spotted from the watchtower.

How to Reach Dobanki Watchtower

Reaching the Dobanki Watchtower is part of the adventure when visiting Sundarban. It involves both road and river transport, making it an exciting journey for tourists.

  • By Air: The nearest airport is Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport in Kolkata. From the airport, you can take a car to Godkhali, which is the starting point for boat rides into Sundarban.
  • By Train: The closest railway station is Canning, located about 48 km from Sundarban. From Canning, you can take a taxi or bus to Godkhali.
  • By Road: The drive from Kolkata to Godkhali takes about 2 hours. Once you reach Godkhali, youâ€™ll board a boat that will take you deep into the mangroves, including a stop at Dobanki Watchtower.

Best Time to Visit Dobanki Watchtower

To make the most of your visit to Dobanki Watchtower, timing is key. Hereâ€™s a breakdown of the best seasons to visit:

  • Winter (November to February): This is the ideal time to visit Dobanki, as the weather is cool and pleasant, making wildlife more active and visible. The chances of tiger sightings are also higher during this season.
  • Monsoon (July to September): While the lush greenery during the monsoon season is stunning, heavy rains can make boat travel challenging, and wildlife sightings may be limited due to thick foliage.
  • Summer (March to June): The summer heat can be intense, but it also brings out wildlife, especially near water sources. Visitors during this time may see animals like deer and wild boars searching for water.

Nearby Attractions to Dobanki Watchtower

While Dobanki Watchtower is a key highlight in Sundarban, there are several other nearby attractions worth exploring during your visit:

  • 1. Sudhanyakhali Watchtower: Located nearby, this watchtower is known for offering excellent chances of spotting Royal Bengal Tigers and other wildlife such as wild boars and saltwater crocodiles.
  • 2. Sajnekhali Watchtower: This watchtower is one of the most visited spots in Sundarban, offering great opportunities for birdwatching and observing the unique mangrove ecosystem.
  • 3. Bhagabatpur Crocodile Project: A crocodile breeding center where visitors can learn about the conservation of these giant reptiles.
  • 4. Netidhopani Watchtower: Known for its mystical 400-year-old temple ruins and the possibility of tiger sightings.

Tips for Visiting Dobanki Watchtower

  • Bring binoculars to enhance your wildlife viewing experience, especially from the canopy walk.

  • Respect nature by not littering and following the guidelines provided by forest authorities.
  • Stay silent and patient to increase your chances of spotting wildlife.
